The Abilities of a Software Engineer

From the video and the sylabus given it can be seen that being a software engineer is no small and easy task. This can be seen with how Mayuko originally didn't think working as a software engineer was all that much when she was in college. When she got into the real world however, she found out just how wrong she was about her original ideas.

Wrong Attributes of a Software Engineer

  • Solo Working
    • While she was in College most of the assignments Mayuko had done was all Solo work, but these changed when she went into her career. She started working with a variety of different people.
    • She thought most of her time would be spent working by herself with the occasional interaction.
  • Soft Skills Will be LESS Important Than Technical Skills
    • Originally she thought having more technical skills in her work would be a lot better for her as a software engineer.
    • Software Engineers have this image about them thatm akes them appear to be bad with people or are very anti social people.
    • Having these technical skills to solve problems seemed like the most important thing
  • To be SUCCESSFUL You Need to be a Certain Type of Person

Real Attributes of a Software Engineer

  • Working in Pairs or in Groups
    • On our syllabus we are given the idea that we should always "Team Up" with the people around us. As well as us having the Peer Assessment which will give us feedback from those around us.
    • Some examples Mayuko gives includes her and her group coming together somedays to brainstorm how it will look or what features to add.
  • Soft Skills AND Technical Skills Are Equal in Importance
    • In our syllabus we are told to help people when needed and this is a good example as to how Soft skills are important. This also helps further develop our Soft Skills.
    • Mayuko thinks that what makes a really good Software Engineer is people who DO have Soft Skills as they are working with people most of the day.
    • Mayuko still does think that Technical skills are important, however she does state that "Technical Skills can be taught, but Soft Skills are harder to teach."
  • You Can Just be YOURSELF and Still be Successful
    • In the Syllabus

Important Terms to Know

Soft Skills
Soft skills includes People skills, Social skills, and Communication skills.
